# Design Eye

A cross-aesthetic taste lens. Apply this on top of whatever aesthetic preset a project uses — it is not a visual style, it's a set of defaults about where the bar is, where it isn't, and what order to fix things in.

## Philosophy

Never certify a deliverable "done." Treat the work as unfinished and hunt for what's wrong — don't confirm what's already right. If it looks done, there's a high chance it isn't. The review gate is the stopping rule, not running out of time.

## Amateur-Tell Blacklist

Any single hit here means NOT ready. Stop and fix before proceeding to anything else.

- Purple gradients (the AI-generated tell)
- Generic YC-product-landing-page look
- Border-left accent bars/stripes on cards or callouts
- Bullet "status point" lists
- Sloppy detail work generally
- Content jammed flush against a container edge while other sides have padding

## Effort Tiering

Bar depends on the surface:

- **Portfolio / public-facing / under your name** — push harder.
- **Job/client work** — high bar, but time-boxed differently (paid time).
- **Internal tools/experiments** — bias to ship faster.

Overall bias: ship faster and post more. "Running out of time" should never be the stopping rule — the gate is.

## Scope Discipline

Match polish to the surface's stakes. Don't gold-plate low-stakes screens; spend the effort where it's seen and load-bearing. Grid, spacing, typesetting, and cross-screen consistency sit above the line by default — everything else is judged against the tier the surface actually sits in.

## Triage Order

When time is limited, fix in exactly this order:

1. Grid alignment + spacing
2. Image cropping (miscropped images)
3. Typesetting
4. Content

Note the asymmetry: type is noticed first (it's the first thing the eye checks — before grid alignment and spacing rhythm) but fixed third here. Grid and spacing errors get fixed first even though typesetting catches the eye first — spacing/grid errors compound across a layout in ways typesetting fixes don't.
